I missed the April update because a lot was going on. So here's a combined catch-up for April and May (so far) — and honestly, this one is all about the app.

## On This Day Rewind - Launched!

The biggest thing that happened in these two months: I finally launched [On This Day Rewind](https://flohgro.com/onthisday) on the App Store in early April! After months of development, TestFlight testing, and a couple of App Review back-and-forths, it was finally out there. Writing the [launch blog post](https://flohgro.com/blog/launching-on-this-day) and hitting "publish" felt surreal. My first iOS app, publicly available.

The response was really encouraging. I definitely didn't expect a mention on [MacStories](https://www.macstories.net/reviews/on-this-day-my-new-favorite-way-to-revisit-old-photos/) on launch day. Then two German Apple blogs picked up the app and reviewed it a couple of weeks later which caused a nice spike in downloads. Thanks to [iphoneblog.de](https://www.iphoneblog.de/2026/05/04/das-datum-steht-wahrend-die-jahreszahl-wechselt-on-this-day-fur-fotoerinnerungen/) and [iPhone-ticker.de](https://www.iphone-ticker.de/on-this-day-rewind-foto-rueckblick-zum-aktuellen-datum-276546/) for the posts. That was a great moment and also got me thinking about how to keep momentum going as an indie developer.

I also already shipped two updates. Version 1.1 added a companion shortcut to open Photos searches, an option to hide screenshots, and fixed a bunch of issues - including photos from further back in time not showing up properly. Version 1.2 then brought a massive performance boost, and a lot of small fixes and polishing based on user feedback.

I'm really enjoying hearing from users, fixing things, and shipping updates. There are already more ideas on my list than I can tackle right now.

Between work, CAMP preparations, house renovations, and family life there is a lot more going on right now — but that's for another post. I would like to write more and will try to pick up some pace on the blog next month, but with summer getting closer that might not happen :).
