Today I'm launching my first app **On This Day Rewind** on the App Store.
It shows you the photos and videos taken on today's date in past years. 

![On This Day Rewind app showing photos grouped by year, a photo detail view with map, and settings]({{ baseUrl }}/assets/on_this_day_screenshot.webp)

Apple Photos does have its own On This Day and Memories features — I use those too. What I wanted was something more intentional: an app where looking back at this specific day is the whole point.

The home screen widget gives you a daily memory at a glance. When you open the app, you get all your photos and videos from today's date grouped by year — and you can share, favorite, or delete them right there, which turns out to be a great way to tidy up your library over time.

Everything runs entirely on your device. No account, no server, no data leaving your phone.

I'm still blown away that [MacStories](https://www.macstories.net/reviews/on-this-day-my-new-favorite-way-to-revisit-old-photos/) (thanks [Devon](https://devondundee.com)) wrote a review of the app -- go read it if you want a proper look at what's in it.

On This Day Rewind is now available on the App Store → [Download here](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/on-this-day-rewind/id6754617354)

## Story Time

This probably started more than ten years ago. I remember sitting on a train on my way home on the weekends during my studies. I used my 2010 MacBook Pro to watch courses about writing apps. When Swift launched in 2014 I was even more excited, but never built something really meaningful. It wasn't the right time and I didn't give it a high enough priority between other obligations.

Fast-forward to more recent years, I had a few conversations where I said I'd really love to create something that people can directly use. In my job we're working on embedded software which is never really experienced by the users. I wanted to get my hands dirty with building an app people can actually use and get in touch with me about it. I always enjoyed the conversations with people using my contributions to the [Drafts Directory](https://directory.getdrafts.com/search?utf8=✓&q=FlohGro) or the early stages of Craft eXtensions. It always felt great when I heard that these were useful for others, too. During a training at work I also committed to others that "someday" I wanted to release an app on the AppStore for the same reason.

And **today is the day**. I can hardly believe that this is really happening, it is a true passion project and also (way too) long in the making. When looking into the git repository I can see my initial commit on January 18, 2024. Back then nothing was really working but two days later my commit message was `seems to work` - I can barely remember how it looked but it was definitely a rough version with many issues. Especially accessing and downloading the images from iCloud broke me several times. However seeing the images popping up there was motivation enough to move on. I kept improving and using the app on my own - it wasn't pretty, it wasn't fast but it was useful enough to keep me hooked. It was always compelling for me to see "what happened on this day in past years" similar to what [Day One](https://dayoneapp.com) offers for journals. I loved looking back to those recent years and see what we did back then. I was especially excited about images from when my wife was pregnant and eagerly awaiting the first birthday of our toddler to see the app's potential when looking back in our family story. 

Then we bought a house and I had to back off for a few months from developing the app further. I kept installing it on my iPhone again and again since the certificate was expiring every week (or so) - this was annoying, but the app was providing enough value for me to do it anyway.

It was magical when we celebrated the first birthday of our son. I could open the app and we looked back at those images from his first day in this world - of course you could scroll back in the photo library (and back then the experience wasn't as good as it is today), but once again I could feel the potential.

When we moved into our new home after a busy summer and a few weeks on vacation I picked it up again and was motivated to adapt the UI for Liquid Glass and iOS 26. Nothing was really straightforward to me especially since I never had to do anything with UI/UX development but this was also part of the journey and things I wanted to learn more about. 

Then one day, I decided that I'd go "all-in" and buy a license for the [Apple Developer Program](https://developer.apple.com/programs/) to create my first TestFlight for the app. It took a few days but on October 29, 2025 I uploaded the first build to App Store Connect. Shortly after I opened it up as a public beta.

I was really happy with the first feedback I got - of course this was a beta version with many rough edges, but the insights from other users were very helpful for me. I added a bunch of new features and fixed a lot of bugs that occurred on other devices. And once again it always hit me when people just told me that they really enjoyed seeing all those memories and they integrated it into their daily routines. 

And after countless hours of working on the app, refining the look and feel, improving the basics and adding more requested features I finally decided that it will be enough for a 1.0 release. On March 19, 2026 I submitted the app for review and I can't tell you how grateful and excited I am to release it today (810 days after my first commit). I want to say **thank you** to everyone who tested the app and provided valuable feedback during the beta time. I couldn't launch it today without you.
